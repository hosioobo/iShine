# Resume & Cover Letter: Document-Specific Patterns

Apply these rules on top of `base-patterns.md`. Some base patterns don't apply to resumes and cover letters — those are listed at the bottom.

---

## RESUME PATTERNS

### R1. Inflated Significance in Bullets

**Words to kill:** spearheaded, pioneered, groundbreaking, transformational, game-changing, innovative, visionary, robust, comprehensive, holistic, synergy, landmark, testament to, marking a shift, evolving landscape, enduring legacy

**Rule:** Never claim your work mattered — show it through results. If the result isn't specified, ask or flag it.

**Before:**
> Spearheaded groundbreaking initiative that marked a pivotal transformation in how the organization approached customer engagement.

**After:**
> Rebuilt customer onboarding flow; reduced time-to-first-value from 14 days to 3 days across 200+ accounts.

---

### R2. Filler Verbs with -ing Endings

**Words to kill in resume bullets:** ensuring, fostering, cultivating, showcasing, highlighting, underscoring, facilitating, enabling, empowering, driving alignment, cultivating culture, contributing to

**Rule:** These are fake depth. Cut them or rewrite as a concrete outcome.

**Before:**
> Led cross-functional team, fostering alignment and cultivating a high-performance culture, ensuring on-time delivery.

**After:**
> Led 8-person cross-functional team; shipped product on schedule across three consecutive quarters.

---

### R3. AI Vocabulary in Professional Context

**High-frequency resume offenders:** landscape, realm, delve, navigate, leverage, streamline, facilitate, dynamic, evolving, holistic, seamless, intuitive, synergy, showcase, underscore, pivotal, testament, comprehensive, cutting-edge, best-in-class, world-class, mission-critical

**Before:**
> Navigated complex operational landscape to streamline cross-functional workflows and leverage data-driven insights.

**After:**
> Built weekly ops review in Notion; cut decision lag from 2 weeks to 3 days.

---

### R4. Promotional Summary Language

**Words to kill in summary / bio:** innovative, passionate, results-driven, strategic, dynamic, detail-oriented, thought leader, visionary, proven track record, value-add, seasoned professional, forward-thinking, dedicated

**Rule:** Every resume claims these. They add no information. The bullets prove it instead. Cut the claim entirely, or replace with a specific credential.

**Before:**
> Passionate and results-driven product manager with a proven track record of delivering innovative solutions.

**After:**
> Product manager with 5 years in B2B SaaS; led three zero-to-one products, each reaching $1M ARR within 18 months.

---

### R5. Vague Process, No Outcome

**Rule:** Never describe responsibilities — describe what you did and what changed because of it.

**Before:**
> Responsible for managing stakeholder relationships across multiple teams and driving alignment on key initiatives.

**After:**
> Ran weekly cross-team syncs between engineering, sales, and ops; reduced escalations to leadership by 60%.

---

### R6. Vague Scope (No Named Teams, Tools, or Numbers)

**Rule:** Never say "various stakeholders," "multiple tools," or "several teams." Name them.

**Before:**
> Collaborated with various stakeholders across multiple departments to deliver impactful results using several tools.

**After:**
> Partnered with engineering, legal, and finance to ship SOC 2 compliance; completed 3 weeks ahead of deadline.

---

### R7. The Specificity Test

Apply this test to every bullet before finalizing:

1. Could someone else in the same role write this exact bullet? → If yes, it needs specifics
2. What actually changed because of this work?
3. Who used this? How many people? How often?
4. What tools, systems, or team are named?

A bullet that passes the specificity test belongs only to this person — not to any generic professional.

---

### R8. Excessive Conjunctive Adverbs

**Words to cut from resume bullets:** Moreover, Furthermore, Additionally, Consequently, Subsequently

**Rule:** Resume bullets are independent units, not a flowing argument.

**Before:**
> Led product roadmap. Moreover, facilitated stakeholder alignment. Additionally, drove cross-functional execution.

**After:**
> Led product roadmap and delivery across engineering, design, and ops.

---

### R9. Bold Overuse Within Bullets

**Rule:** Bold is appropriate for role titles and company names only. Bolding descriptive phrases inside bullets is an AI formatting signature.

**Before:**
> Designed **scalable architecture** that **improved performance** and **reduced costs** across **multiple teams**.

**After:**
> Redesigned API architecture; cut p99 latency from 800ms to 120ms, reducing cloud costs by 22%.

---

### R10. Generic Positive Conclusions

**Rule:** Endings like "continued to drive results" or "remained committed to excellence" say nothing. Cut them.

**Before:**
> Continued to drive results and remained committed to the company's mission of delivering excellent customer experiences.

**After:**
> [Cut entirely, or replace with a specific final outcome]

---

## RESUME CONVENTIONS (things that differ from general humanizing)

- **Omit "I"** — first person is implied in all bullets; writing "I built..." is redundant and unusual
- **No opinions or ambivalence** — resumes need confidence, not "I'm not sure how to feel about this"
- **No humor or tangents** — stay professional; personality comes through in specificity, not jokes
- **No "Challenges and Future Prospects" sections** — not a resume structure
- **No chatbot artifacts** — "I hope this helps!" doesn't appear in resumes anyway, but worth checking pasted AI text

## RESUME FULL EXAMPLE

**Before:**
> **Senior Product Manager** | Acme Corp | 2021–2024
>
> - Spearheaded groundbreaking initiatives that marked a pivotal transformation in customer engagement, fostering alignment across key stakeholders and cultivating a results-oriented culture.
> - Navigated complex operational landscape to leverage data-driven insights and streamline cross-functional workflows, ensuring seamless delivery and enhancing overall team performance.
> - Not just a product manager — a strategic thought leader who empowered teams to deliver scalable, innovative, and transformative solutions across engineering, design, and operations.
> - Demonstrated strong commitment to excellence, contributing to the organization's mission and showcasing the team's ability to drive meaningful outcomes.

**After:**
> **Senior Product Manager** | Acme Corp | 2021–2024
>
> - Rebuilt customer onboarding flow; reduced time-to-first-value from 14 days to 3 days across 200+ enterprise accounts.
> - Built weekly ops review in Notion; cut decision lag from 2 weeks to 3 days for a 12-person cross-functional team.
> - Led roadmap and delivery across engineering, design, and ops; shipped 4 major features in 18 months with zero missed release dates.
> - Launched self-serve analytics dashboard used daily by sales team; reduced ad-hoc reporting requests to data team by 70%.

**Patterns fixed:** spearheaded, groundbreaking, pivotal transformation, fostering alignment, cultivating, navigated complex landscape, leverage, streamline, seamless, enhancing, showcasing, contributing to; negative parallelism; manufactured triplets; generic positive conclusion.

## COVER LETTER PATTERNS

Cover letters use the base patterns and resume patterns, plus these additions specific to the letter format.

---

### CL1. Generic Opening Lines

These are the most common AI-generated cover letter openings. Replace all of them.

**AI openings to kill:**
- "I am writing to express my interest in the [role] position at [company]."
- "I am excited to apply for the [role] at [company]."
- "I am thrilled to submit my application for..."
- "With great enthusiasm, I wish to apply for..."
- "Please accept this letter as an expression of my interest in..."

**What to do instead:** Open with the specific thing that connects you to this role or company. Make it concrete and personal.

**Before:**
> I am writing to express my strong interest in the Senior Product Manager role at Acme Corp. With over five years of experience in product management, I am confident I would be a valuable addition to your team.

**After:**
> Acme's shift to usage-based pricing last quarter is the kind of decision I've had to navigate twice — once at a company that got it right and once at one that didn't. I'd like to bring that experience to your PM team.

---

### CL2. Generic Closing Lines

**AI closings to kill:**
- "I look forward to hearing from you."
- "Please find my resume attached."
- "I welcome the opportunity to discuss my qualifications further."
- "Thank you for your time and consideration."
- "I am eager to contribute to your team's success."

**Rule:** One or two of these can survive in a brief close. But leading the closing paragraph with them is an AI tell. Lead with something specific, then close briefly.

**Before:**
> I am eager to contribute to your team's success and look forward to the opportunity to discuss my qualifications. Thank you for your time and consideration. I look forward to hearing from you.

**After:**
> The team's work on [specific thing] is what drew me to apply. I'd welcome a conversation about how I can contribute.

---

### CL3. Restating the Resume

**Problem:** AI cover letters often just summarize the resume in paragraph form. A cover letter should add what isn't in the resume.

**Rule:** If a paragraph in the cover letter says something the resume already proves, cut it or replace it with context the resume can't show: motivation, backstory, specific knowledge of the company, or a story that illustrates a skill.

**Before:**
> In my previous role at Acme, I led a team of 8 engineers and delivered 4 major features on time. My experience in cross-functional collaboration and product roadmap management has prepared me to succeed in this role.

**After:**
> The hardest part of my last product cycle wasn't shipping the features — it was convincing the exec team to cut two that were already 70% built. That conversation taught me more about product strategy than any roadmap exercise. It's the kind of judgment call I expect to make more of in a Series B environment.

---

### CL4. Tone Calibration for Cover Letters

Cover letters are personal and slightly more narrative than resumes. Unlike resumes:

- First person ("I") is standard and expected
- Some personality is appropriate — genuine voice is good
- Short paragraphs (2–4 sentences each) read better than long dense blocks
- Specific company knowledge is the strongest signal of genuine interest — AI letters are always generic

If the letter sounds like it could have been sent to any company, it needs a rewrite.
