# Marketing Copy: Document-Specific Patterns

Apply these rules on top of `base-patterns.md`. Marketing copy is intentionally persuasive — that's different from being inflated or vague. The goal here is not to make marketing sound neutral. It's to make it sound like it was written by a human who actually knows the product and who they're writing to.

---

## WHAT'S DIFFERENT ABOUT MARKETING COPY

Marketing copy is allowed to:

- Be enthusiastic and promotional
- Use short, punchy sentences
- Make bold claims — **as long as they're specific**
- Have a clear point of view on the product
- Address the reader directly ("you")

The difference between good marketing copy and AI marketing copy is **specificity**. AI copy makes generic promises. Good copy names exact problems, exact outcomes, and exact people.

---

## THE CORE MARKETING FAILURE MODE

AI marketing copy sounds like every other product in its category. It uses the same power words, the same benefit statements, the same structure. The result is copy that could have been written for any product — which means it doesn't sell any specific one.

**The test:** Could this exact sentence appear in a competitor's marketing? If yes, it needs rewriting.

---

## MARKETING-SPECIFIC AI PATTERNS TO FIX

### M1. Generic Transformation Claims

AI loves to promise "transformation" without saying what changes, for whom, or by how much.

**Words to kill:** transform, revolutionize, reimagine, reinvent, redefine, game-changing, paradigm shift, elevate, unlock, supercharge, unleash

**Before:**
> Transform the way your team works. Unleash productivity. Elevate your business to the next level.

**After:**
> Cut approval cycles from 5 days to same-day. Your team stops waiting; your clients stop chasing.

---

### M2. Vague Benefit Statements

AI names benefit categories instead of actual benefits.

**Patterns to fix:**
- "Improve efficiency" → By how much? In what process?
- "Save time" → How much time? On what?
- "Boost productivity" → Define productivity. Who measures it?
- "Enhance collaboration" → What specifically becomes easier?
- "Streamline your workflow" → Which workflow? What does streamlined mean?
- "Drive results" → What results? For whom?

**Rule:** Every benefit claim should answer: for whom, in what situation, with what specific outcome.

**Before:**
> Our platform streamlines your workflow and improves team efficiency, helping you drive better results across your organization.

**After:**
> Marketing teams at mid-size agencies use it to cut brief-to-publish time from 11 days to 4 — without adding headcount.

---

### M3. AI Power Word Overload

These words are statistically overused in AI-generated marketing. When several appear together, it's an instant tell.

**High-frequency offenders:** seamless, intuitive, powerful, robust, comprehensive, holistic, effortless, cutting-edge, next-generation, world-class, best-in-class, enterprise-grade, innovative, dynamic, scalable, future-proof, industry-leading, award-winning, turnkey, plug-and-play

**Before:**
> Our seamless, intuitive platform provides robust, comprehensive solutions that are scalable, future-proof, and enterprise-grade.

**After:**
> The platform connects to your existing stack in under an hour. No custom dev work, no professional services required.

---

### M4. "Designed For" Filler

AI writing often says a product is "designed for" or "built for" without explaining what specifically it does.

**Before:**
> Built for modern teams. Designed for the way you work.

**After:**
> Built for ops teams who've outgrown spreadsheets but can't justify a full ERP.

---

### M5. Fake Urgency and Manufactured Scarcity

These patterns signal desperation more than value. They're also immediately recognizable as AI-generated filler.

**Before:**
> Don't miss out on this limited-time opportunity. Act now before it's too late! Join thousands of satisfied customers who are already transforming their workflows.

**After:**
> Pricing increases in Q2 as we add the dedicated support tier. Current plans are locked in at today's rate.

---

### M6. Social Proof Without Specificity

AI testimonials and social proof are always vague. Real social proof names the person, company, role, and specific outcome.

**Before:**
> "This product changed the way we work. Highly recommended!" — Happy Customer

**After:**
> "We cut our weekly reporting from 6 hours to 45 minutes. It used to require three people; now one person runs it before lunch." — Jane Smith, Head of Revenue Operations, Greenfield Analytics

---

### M7. Features List Masquerading as Benefits

AI marketing copy often lists features in a way that sounds like benefits but provides no actual benefit.

**Before:**
> Features include:
> - Advanced analytics dashboard
> - Real-time collaboration
> - Enterprise-grade security
> - Seamless integrations

**After:**
> See exactly which campaigns drove revenue — not just clicks — in a dashboard your CFO can read. Edits sync live so your team stops overwriting each other. Connects to Salesforce, HubSpot, and 40+ other tools without an IT ticket.

---

### M8. The Problem-Agitate-Solve Formula Gone Wrong

PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve) is a legitimate copywriting framework. AI versions describe the problem so generically that nobody recognizes themselves in it.

**Before:**
> Are you struggling to keep up with the demands of modern business? In today's competitive landscape, teams face increasing pressure to do more with less. If you're feeling overwhelmed, you're not alone.

**After:**
> Your team is underwater in Slack. The person who owns this process is in back-to-back meetings until 4pm. By the time they're free to respond, the decision is already late — so someone makes a guess, or nobody does anything.

---

### M9. CTAs That Ask for Nothing Specific

Generic CTAs tell the reader to do something without giving them a reason why now.

**Weak CTAs:**
- "Get started today"
- "Learn more"
- "Sign up for free"
- "Try it now"
- "See how it works"

**What makes a CTA work:** It names the specific thing the reader will get or experience, in their language.

**Before:**
> Ready to transform your business? Get started today!

**After:**
> See your team's approval cycle mapped in 10 minutes. No setup needed.

---

### M10. Missing the Specific Audience

AI marketing copy addresses "teams," "organizations," or "businesses" — categories so broad they include no one in particular.

**Before:**
> Perfect for teams of all sizes, from startups to enterprises. Whether you're a small business or a large corporation, our solution scales to fit your needs.

**After:**
> Works best for 10–50-person ops and finance teams who've hit the ceiling on what spreadsheets can track. Not built for enterprise — no dedicated IT onboarding, no SSO.

## TONE CALIBRATION FOR MARKETING

### What AI marketing sounds like: confident but empty

AI copy is grammatically smooth but has no texture. It claims everything and promises nothing specific. Every product sounds the same.

### What good marketing sounds like: specific and opinionated

Good marketing copy knows its product's strengths AND its weaknesses, and it chooses who it's for. The best landing pages say "this isn't for you if..." as clearly as they say "this is for you if..."

### The brand voice problem

AI marketing copy sounds like "marketing." It doesn't sound like a specific brand. If the product has an established voice — dry humor, technical precision, warmth, bluntness — preserve it. If there's no existing voice, aim for clarity and specificity over smoothness.

### Length calibration

Marketing copy should be as long as it needs to be to overcome objections and no longer. AI copy pads length with vague claims. Cut ruthlessly — if a sentence doesn't say something specific, it probably shouldn't be there.

## MARKETING FULL EXAMPLE

**Before:**
> Introducing the next-generation platform for modern teams. Our seamless, intuitive solution empowers organizations to streamline their workflows, enhance collaboration, and drive meaningful results. With enterprise-grade security and comprehensive integrations, you can transform the way your team works — effortlessly.
>
> Don't miss out. Get started today and unlock the full potential of your business.

**After:**
> Most project management tools were built for software teams. This one was built for agencies juggling 30+ clients at once.
>
> You get a single view across every client's work — status, blockers, next action — without switching tabs or asking anyone for updates. Clients get a portal that makes it look like you have twice the headcount.
>
> Used by 400+ agencies. Fastest adoption in teams that have outgrown ClickUp.
>
> See your agency's workflow in 15 minutes. No credit card.

**What changed:** Removed "next-generation," "seamless," "intuitive," "empower," "streamline," "meaningful results," "enterprise-grade," "transform," "effortlessly," "unlock full potential." Added specific audience (agencies, 30+ clients), specific feature descriptions, specific social proof (400+ agencies, ClickUp comparison), specific CTA (15 minutes, no credit card). Copy now has a point of view and a defined audience.
